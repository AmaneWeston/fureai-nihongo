Fureai Nihongo no Kai (The Group of Japanese Teachers) was established in 1991. All members of our teaching staff had completed a course of study in the teaching of the Japanese language and obtained teaching certificates. Herewith, a chronology of our group.
1991

Started voluntary Japanese lessons to two families of refugees from Vietnam. (~1995)

Japanese lessons to the businessmen of a major company in Kamakura. (~2000)

1992

Started voluntary Japanese lessons to Brazilian workers.

1993

“Japanese Conversation Course (10 lessons)”

1994

“Japanese Conversation Course (20 lessons)” sponsored by Kamakura City.

1995

Received “The Avon Group Support Prize”

“Japanese Conversation Course(20 lessons)” sponsored by Kamakura City.

“The 1st Japanese Speech Contest for Foreigners” supported by Kamakura City.

(The 2nd Contest in 1996, The 3rd Contest in 1998)

1996

Started Japanese lessons to students studying abroad at Kamakura High School and Ofuna High School.

1997

Started voluntary Japanese lessons to British “gap year” volunteers at St. Theresa Hospital.(~2002)

1998

“Japanese Cooking Class for Foreigners” (2 lessons, 35participants)

1999

Started Japanese lessons at The British Embassy Language School.(~2006)

Japanese lessons to a Chinese boy at Shichirigahama Elementary School.

2000

Started Japanese lessons to students studying abroad at Kamakura High School (~2004) and Shichirigahama High School. (~2016)

2013

Started voluntary Japanese lessons at NPO Kamakura Center. (~2018)

2017

Japanese lessons to a student studying abroad at Zushi Kaisei Junior and Senior High School.(~2018)

To date we have taught Japanese to more than 300 students from 35 countries. U.S.A., U.K., Israel, India, Indonesia, Ukraine, Australia, Austria, Canada, Korea, Kyrgyzstan, Switzerland, Sweden, Spain, SriLanka, Thailand, Taiwan, Czech, China, Germany, Turkey, Nepal, NewZealand, Haiti, Hungary, Philippines, Brazil, France, Vietnam, Poland, Portugal, South Africa, Mexico, Lebanon, and Russia.
